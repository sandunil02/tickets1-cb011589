Hikka Turtle Hatchery

Where it all began

Hikkaduwa, a town on the coast of Sri Lanka, is home to the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, which has a long history of protecting sea turtles. The hatchery has been instrumental since its start in the effort to save critically endangered sea turtles. In the face of dangers including habitat destruction, pollution, and poaching, the hatchery has been instrumental in protecting nesting locations, moving eggs to safe havens, and raising young until they are ready for release. The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery has made tremendous strides in the conservation of sea turtles thanks to its partnerships with local people and the persistent work of its dedicated employees.
