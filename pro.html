Our Mission

Our mission is to promote the preservation of Sri Lanka's sea turtles and their habitats, to raise awareness of the importance of turtle hatcheries, and to empower individuals to make a difference in their conservation efforts.

Sri Lankan Turtle population

Green turtles, loggerheads, hawksbills, olive ridleys, and leatherbacks are the five kinds of marine turtles found in Sri Lanka. Sri Lankans place both cultural and economic value on these turtles, since they are an integral element of the country's marine environment. Poaching, the destruction of turtle habitat, pollution, and unintentional ensnarement in fishing nets are only some of the dangers faced by Sri Lanka's turtle population. In order to save the turtles and their eggs, many conservation programs and turtle hatcheries have been set up in different parts of the nation. These campaigns have increased turtle numbers and brought attention to the need of conservation.





What is a turtle hatchery?

A turtle hatchery is a facility that is designed to protect and incubate sea turtle eggs until they hatch and the baby turtles emerge. Sea turtles typically come ashore at night to lay their eggs in the sand, and the hatchery provides a safe and controlled environment for the eggs to incubate, protecting them from predators, natural disasters, and human disturbance. Once the eggs hatch, the baby turtles are often kept in the hatchery for a short period of time to give them a better chance of survival. They may be monitored and protected from predators until they are strong enough to make their way to the ocean. Hatcheries may also provide educational opportunities for visitors to learn about sea turtles, their life cycle, and the importance of conservation efforts to protect them.








Turtle Hatcheries in Sri Lanka




Ahangama

Ahangama

The Ahangama Turtle Hatchery is a protection project in Sri Lanka that started in 1996. Its goal is to protect sea turtles and their eggs from thieves and other pests.

Ambalangoda

Ambalangoda

Founded the hatchery near Ambalangoda on Sri Lanka's southern coast in 1986. The hatchery buys turtle eggs from local fisherman and cares for them until they hatch.

Galle

Galle

This was founded in 1996 at Galle, Sri Lanka's ancient southern port. The hatchery buys turtle eggs from local fisherman and preserves them until they hatch, then releases them into the ocean.

Koggala

Koggala

The Turtle Conservation Project set up the hatchery in 1996. It is in the village of Koggala, which is on the southern coast of Sri Lanka.

Importance of Turtle hatcheries

  • Conservation: Hatcheries protect endangered turtle species from threats like habitat loss and poaching.
  • Nest Protection: Hatcheries safeguard nests from predators and human disturbance.
  • Hatchling Survival:Controlled conditions in hatcheries increase survival rates.
  • Release Programs: Hatcheries reintroduce hatchlings to their natural habitats, promoting population growth and genetic diversity.
  • Public Awareness: Hatcheries educate the public about turtle conservation and encourage responsible actions.
  • Research and Monitoring: Hatcheries provide opportunities for scientific study and population monitoring.
  • Economic Benefits: Turtle hatcheries boost local economies through ecotourism and job creation.
  • Ecosystem Balance: By conserving turtle populations, hatcheries contribute to the health and stability of marine and coastal ecosystems.

What are unique features of turtles?

  1. Shell: Turtles have a distinctive shell consisting of a carapace and a plastron, providing protection and defense against predators.
  2. Limbs and Gait: Turtles have specialized limbs adapted for their semi-aquatic lifestyle, with flippers or webbed feet. Their unique gait is characterized by slow and steady movements due to the weight of their shells.
  3. Long Lifespan: Turtles exhibit remarkable longevity, with many species living for several decades or even over a hundred years, thanks to their slow metabolism and well-protected shells.
  4. Adaptations for Aquatic Life: Aquatic turtles possess adaptations such as streamlined bodies, webbed feet, and the ability to extract oxygen from water, allowing them to thrive in aquatic environments.



These features distinguish turtles and contribute to their survival and success in various habitats.
