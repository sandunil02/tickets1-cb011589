The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a well-known conservation project in the Sri Lankan town of Kosgoda. Its goal is to protect and preserve sea turtles. The nursery was set up because the number of sea turtles was going down because of different risks. It is very important to the life of these interesting animals.

The main goal of the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is to keep an eye on nesting sea turtles, collect their eggs, and make sure they have a safe place to hatch and grow up before they are released. The school works closely with local people, tourists, and workers to spread the word about sea turtle protection and how important it is to protect their natural environments.

Visitors to the hatchery can learn about the different kinds of sea turtles that live in Sri Lanka, such as the Green turtle, the Olive Ridley turtle, the Loggerhead turtle, the Hawksbill turtle, and the Leatherback turtle. The staff at the farm knows a lot about how sea turtles live, where they lay their eggs, and what dangers they face in the wild.
