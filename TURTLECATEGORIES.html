Turtle Categories in Sri lanka

Green~TURTLE

The Green Turtle is the most commonly found species in Sri Lanka and can be found nesting on beaches along the country's southern and western coasts. Some of the most popular nesting sites for Green Turtles in Sri Lanka include the beaches of Kosgoda, Rekawa, and Induruwa.

Oliveridley~TURTLE

The Olive Ridley Turtle is the smallest of the five species found in Sri Lanka and can be found nesting on the country's eastern and western coasts. The beaches of Panama, Okanda, and Kallady are some of the most important nesting sites for Olive Ridley Turtles in Sri Lanka.

Hawksbill~TURTLE

The Hawksbill Turtle is a critically endangered species that is known for its beautiful shell, which is used to make jewelry and other decorative items. Hawksbill Turtles can be found nesting on beaches along the southern and eastern coasts of Sri Lanka. The beaches of Rekawa, Induruwa, and Hikkaduwa are some of the most important nesting sites for Hawksbill Turtles in Sri Lanka.

Loggerhead~TURTLE

The Loggerhead Turtle is named for its large head and strong jaws, which it uses to feed on crabs and other hard-shelled prey. Loggerhead Turtles can be found nesting on beaches along the southern and western coasts of Sri Lanka. The beaches of Kosgoda, Rekawa, and Bentota are some of the most important nesting sites for Loggerhead Turtles in Sri Lanka.

Leatherback~TURTLE

he Leatherback Turtle is the largest of the five species found in Sri Lanka, reaching up to seven feet in length and weighing up to 2,000 pounds. Leatherback Turtles can be found nesting on beaches along the country's eastern and southern coasts. The beaches of Panama, Arugam Bay, and Pigeon Island are some of the most important nesting sites for Leatherback Turtles in Sri Lanka.
